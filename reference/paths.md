# The 22 Paths

The network that connects the 10 Sefirot. Each path *is* one Hebrew letter,
which *is* one Major Arcanum. To travel a path, play its card.

Paths traverse in either direction. The game is an ascent (Malkuth → Kether),
but the numbering is traditional top-down.

## Notable sub-structures

### The central pillar (Path of the Arrow)
`Malkuth → Yesod → Tiferet → Kether` — three paths: **The World, Temperance,
The High Priestess**. The direct route. Shortest and hardest.

### The three horizontal paths ("abyss-crossings")
- **Path 14** — Chokmah ↔ Binah (Empress) — upper / widest
- **Path 19** — Chesed ↔ Gevurah (Strength) — middle
- **Path 27** — Netzach ↔ Hod (Tower) — lower

Crossing between pillars changes which stats get emphasized next.

### The three paths out of Malkuth
The opening moves. Players choose direction from the very first turn.
- **Path 32** — The World → Yesod (gentle)
- **Path 31** — Judgement → Hod (left pillar)
- **Path 29** — The Moon → Netzach (right pillar)

### The three paths into Kether
The final climb. At least one of these three cards must be in the team's
hands to win.
- **Path 11** — The Fool → from Chokmah
- **Path 12** — The Magician → from Binah
- **Path 13** — The High Priestess → from Tiferet

## "Lightning Flash" — the descent

For reference, the traditional downward zigzag by which divine energy
emanates through all ten Sefirot, in order:

`Kether → Chokmah → Binah → Chesed → Gevurah → Tiferet → Netzach → Hod → Yesod → Malkuth`

The Lightning Flash is a Sefirot-sequence, not a path-sequence. Players in
Sparks of Kether traverse its **reverse**, but rarely in strict order — the
path network offers many weavings.
