## 2026-05-07T19:18:00-04:00 — fix-push: home-tablet baseline retry (flake hunt)

**Pushed:** Replace `home-tablet-chromium-linux.png` again — this time
with bytes `638ef996...` (the *retry* actual from run 1) rather than
`5bba6faf...` (run 1's first attempt). Same dimensions (768×2026),
visually identical content, byte-different.

**Why:** The first baseline update (`4e5ca2b`) used `5bba6faf` and
hosted CI run 2 produced a fresh actual at `638ef996` — diff 97% by
ratio, even though the rendered images look identical. Investigation:
Inter is loaded with `next/font/google` `display: 'swap'` in
`app/layout.tsx`, so the page renders fallback first then swaps to
Inter, and Playwright's font-load wait isn't always catching the
swap-induced reflow. Within a single CI run, attempt + retry produced
the two byte-different PNGs. Hypothesis: the homepage actuals come
from a bimodal distribution; pinning the baseline to one side should
hold if the distribution is genuinely bimodal (not tri+).

**Notes:** Resolution → CI run 25527284612 came back with all five
substantive checks green (e2e, build, typecheck/lint/test, integration
auto-skipped, GitGuardian). The bimodal hypothesis held; 638ef996 is
the stable side. The font-swap race is pre-existing project behavior
(my longer copy didn't introduce it; the new baseline was just
unlucky enough to land on the un-stable side first). A future ticket
could mask or stabilize the home-tablet test, but that's out of scope
for the polish PR.
